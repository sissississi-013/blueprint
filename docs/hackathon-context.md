## The Challenge

Build a useful / interesting **AI agent for business or corporate use cases** using the required stack. Other use cases are allowed, but business/corporate are preferred. All AI workloads must run **locally** on the Dell Pro Max with GB10 provided on-site, and the final demo must also run on that machine.

## Required Stack

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| Stack | **OpenClaw + NVIDIA NemoClaw + OpenShell** |
| Hardware | Dell Pro Max with GB10 (provided on-site, one per team) |
| Local-first | All AI workload runs locally on the box. Final demo runs on the box. You may code on your own laptop. |

**NemoClaw** is NVIDIA's open-source reference stack for running always-on OpenClaw assistants safely. It installs **OpenShell**, a sandboxed runtime designed for executing agents with additional security, alongside open-source NVIDIA Nemotron models. Everything runs locally on the Dell Pro Max with GB10, with no host filesystem or network exposure.

## Rules

**The short version:** Build an interesting AI agent. Run it locally on the Dell Pro Max with GB10. Ship inside the day.

1. **Build window** — The build sprint runs on the day, on-site, on the provided GB10 hardware. The agent itself must be built during the event.
2. **Stack requirements** — All AI workload must run locally on the box. Required components: OpenClaw + NVIDIA NemoClaw + OpenShell. You can code on your own laptop, but the final demo must run on the GB10.
3. **Use case** — Submission must be a working AI agent. Business or corporate use cases preferred, but other uses allowed.
4. **Teams** — Hard cap of 40 teams; each team gets exactly one box, no exceptions. Teams of 2–4 builders. Solo entries not accepted. Team formation partly managed by organizers. Each builder registers individually on BuilderBase.
5. **Submission** — Submit through the BuilderBase submission portal. Required: a working demo running on the GB10.
6. **Scoring** — Top 8 are selected from all submissions, then deliver short pitches. Rubric includes a bonus for smaller teams (e.g. a team of 2 gets more bonus points than a team of 5).
7. **Hardware** — Each team is loaned one GB10 plus a monitor and keyboard/mouse for the day.
8. **Prizes** — Awarded per team, not per builder. 1st: one GB10. 2nd & 3rd: one Dell laptop per team. Finalists 5–8: swag from the Dell rewards catalog per team.

## FAQ

### Logistics
- **Where and when?** San Francisco, one day. Roughly 9 AM start, awards by 8 PM PT. Venue confirmed to registered builders.
- **In-person or remote?** In-person only — the point is hands-on time with the GB10 hardware. You must be physically in San Francisco.
- **Cost?** Free. Food and drinks covered.

### Eligibility & Teams
- **Who can register?** Anyone who wants to build on the GB10 with the OpenClaw + NVIDIA NemoClaw + OpenShell stack. Technical builders preferred; open to students, developers, operators.
- **How many teams?** 40, hard cap. 40 GB10 units, one box per team.
- **Team size?** 2–4 builders. Solos aren't accepted but will be paired.
- **Will my team stay intact?** Tell organizers at registration; they'll do their best, but the 40-team cap takes priority. Teams with empty seats may be paired with solo builders, always with advance notice.
- **Bring own hardware?** No. Every team gets a GB10 plus monitor and keyboard/mouse. You can bring your own laptop for dev, but the demo runs on the box.
- **Should I bring anything?** Boxes come ready to log in, but nothing else is pre-installed. Bring specific models, libraries, or assets on a flash drive — faster than day-of internet.

### Build & Submission
- **What stack?** OpenClaw + NVIDIA NemoClaw + OpenShell on the GB10.
- **Where does inference run?** Locally on the box. All AI workload must run on the GB10. Code on your own laptop, but the final demo runs on the GB10.
- **What kind of agent?** "Some sort of interesting agent." Business/corporate use cases preferred, but the door is open.

### Prizes & Judging
- **How does judging work?** Narrow submissions to a top 8, who deliver short pitches; then pick a top 3. Rubric includes a small bonus for smaller teams.
- **Does every builder on the winning team get a prize?** No — prizes are per team, not per builder. A winning team of 2–4 shares one GB10. Runners-up: one Dell laptop per 2nd/3rd place team, regardless of size.
- **When do I find out if I won?** Awards announced live at the closing ceremony.
